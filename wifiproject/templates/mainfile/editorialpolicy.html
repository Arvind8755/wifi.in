Editorial Policy | Accuracy, Transparency & Source Verification – WifiResult

Purpose

WifiResult is committed to providing accurate, timely and trustworthy information about government job notifications, admit cards, results and related exam details. This Editorial Policy explains how we collect, verify, publish and correct information on our site.

Core Principles

  • Accuracy: We prioritise official and primary sources for every job notification and update we publish.
  • Transparency: We clearly cite the source of each notification and provide links to the original official documents where available.
  • Independence: Our editorial decisions are independent of advertisers; sponsored content is clearly labelled.
  • Responsiveness: We correct factual errors promptly and document corrections when they occur.

Sources & Verification

We verify all job notifications and related announcements primarily from official government channels before publishing. These include:

  • Official organisation websites (e.g., ibps.in, ssc.nic.in, indiapost.gov.in, state government portals).
  • Official press releases and circulars issued by ministries, departments, and recruiting agencies.
  • Official social media handles of government agencies (where the announcement is linked back to the agency website or official PDF).
  • Gazette notifications and other legal publications when applicable.

Verification steps we follow:

  1. Locate the announcement on the official site or official PDF/circular.
  2. Cross-check key details (vacancy count, eligibility, important dates, application link) against the official notice.
  3. When possible, link directly to the official notification PDF or web page in the article ("Official Notification" link).
  4. If an official source is not available, we explicitly state the source and mark the information as provisional until official confirmation.

Corrections & Updates

If we or our readers identify a factual error, we promptly investigate and correct the content. Our corrections follow these steps:

  • Verify the correction with the official source.
  • Update the article with the corrected information and note the change (date and brief reason) at the top or bottom of the article.
  • When an official retraction or change occurs (e.g., revised vacancy numbers or exam dates), we update the article and keep a short correction log for transparency.

Conflicts of Interest & Sponsored Content

Our editorial team operates independently of commercial partners. Any sponsored or paid content is clearly labelled as such. Team members must disclose conflicts of interest and recuse themselves from coverage where appropriate.

How to Report an Issue

If you find an error, missing official link, or have a concern about an article, please contact us with details:

  • Email: wifiresult3@gmail.com
  • Provide: Article URL, specific issue, and links to official source (if available).

We will acknowledge receipt and respond with our findings or action within 24–48 hours on working days.

Editorial Team & Review

Our editorial team consists of experienced editors and subject-matter contributors focusing on recruitment notices. Content is reviewed by at least one editor before publishing. For high-impact or ambiguous notices (e.g., large-scale national recruitments), additional senior review is performed.

Privacy & Data

When users contact us to report issues or corrections, we collect only the information necessary to address the concern (name, email, message). We do not share personal contact details with third parties except when required by law. For more, see our Privacy Policy (link to be added).

Limitations

While we strive for accuracy, web content and government notices can change. Always consult the official notice and the recruiting organisation’s website for final and legally binding information (e.g., exact dates, eligibility and application procedure).

Our Commitment

WifiResult is dedicated to delivering clear, verified and useful recruitment information. We verify data with official government sources and maintain an open corrections policy to preserve trust and transparency with our readers.
